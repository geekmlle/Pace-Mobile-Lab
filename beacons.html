Beacons

By Jan Schoepp

Have you ever thought to yourself “I wish I could get a notification whenever I’m close to something that’s on sale”, or “I wish I could get notified when the next bus is getting close”. Well the technology you’re thinking of already exists and it’s called beacons!

Beacons can come in many different shapes and sizes, but are generally small wireless sensors that you can attach to any location or object. They broadcast tiny radio signals using Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) which your smartphone can receive and interpret, unlocking micro-location and contextual awareness. This means that if you’re entering a shop that has utilized beacons, you can get notified when you are close to an item you’re looking for, or if there is a sale nearby!

This technology is available to everyone and with the use of SDKs, such as Estimote, you can make apps on your smartphone that are able to understand their proximity to nearby locations and objects, recognizing their type, ownership, approximate location, temperature and motion. Using this data allows us to build a new generation of magical mobile apps that connect the real world to your smart device. The Estimote SDK allows for app development on both Android and iOS!

To demonstrate the beacon capabilities, Jan Schoepp has prepared a presentation which gives insight into some use-cases for beacons, as well as some technical information, and there is also a tutorial teaching you how to quickly code your own beacon-compatible app!

Also featured is an app that will notify you as you enter the Common Area at the Seidenberg School of Computing, and as you open the app you will be able to see if there are any tutors available at the time, as well as their name and email address.

Check out the code on Github and Prezi presentation today!
